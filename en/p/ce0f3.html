Winter is coming

“Winter is coming.” She looks uneasily to the sky.
“But it’s spring…” Zote didn’t know what she meant and also didn’t care. “… If there is anything the great almighty Zote can do for you, ma’am, please let me know.”
She smiled politely. “I will, Knight Zote.”
A pale pink glow scattered around her claws, her kimono fluttering in the cold wind outside the window.
In the early hours of the next morning, snow began to fall. Seeing snow for the first time, the newly born little net-bugs ran out of the hut and caught it with their coats. Zote, awakened by the excited squeals of the bugs, groaned in a low voice, and was rushed back into his room by the cold air as soon as he walked out the door. He rubbed his claws around the lighted stove.
Sakura looked gloomier than yesterday, her saber-eyebrows frowning, sitting on the birch floor looking out the window at the children having snowball fights.
“Knight Zote, I have a favor to ask…”
“Speak up, my lady?”
She went silent, her eyes very sad. “… I… I want you to escort us out of here.”
“Leave? Why?”
“… I can feel it… Something bad is coming.”
“Er… Oh, oh, that, I know, I know.” He patted his chest. “Leave it to me!”
“You know… That thing?”
“That thing? …of course, of course I know. How could a knight not know that? Yes,of course.”
Sakura’s eyes widened. “That… Do you know how to resist him?”
“Ah? … Oh that, I know that too –”
“That’s great! “Sakura smiled.” If you could defend us against him, we wouldn’t have to hide anymore!”
“… Well, “zote said, not keeping up at all,” hide? Why hide?” He tried to change the subject.
“He… He killed my children…” Sakura’s small body began to shiver, “… I’ve lost enough already.”
“He was after me. Just me.” Sakura clenched her claws tightly. “But I can’t die. I can’t leave my people, my children…”
“Knight Zott, can you tell me how to defeat him?”
“… Er, easy, easy. All it takes is one… Ritual is enough.”
“Ritual?”
At night, the wind blew. The bamboo curtains of the wooden house crackled.
Sakura sits quietly in the empty wooden house, the floor of which is covered with strange lines drawn in blood.
suddenly the door of the wooden house was flung open by the strong wind, as snow poured into the darkness.
Sakura began to shake uncontrollably, but she fought back her instinct to flee.
The white snow lay thick, right up to Sakura’s knees.
She had never been so close to death before. The cold smell filled the room, and frost began to condense on the doors and Windows. She’s always been the one being hunted. But this time… Maybe the future will change.
“Wyrm…” Her lips barely moved, the word barely audible, yet it echoed in the silence of the room, “Come… Now.”
Sakura stood up and stepped forward, slowly. She held her arms wide apart, as if trying to embrace something invisible. She closed her eyes and inhaled deeply.
Suddenly Sakura felt the pressure lift from her shoulders, the cold sensation from the blood that pooled under her skin disappeared.
Slowly, carefully, Sakura turned around and looked behind her.
The room was completely dark, save for the fire burning in the wood stove.
Her heart fell, then jumped into her throat.
“Oh god…”
She heard Zote’s laughter echoing in the room.
And she saw him standing there, right behind the Wyrm, smirking.
Wyrm’s robes, though still black, were no longer black.
They were white. Pure white. They gleamed, like snow itself was floating in the air.
The White Cloak floated above her head, just like the cloak she once wore on her back, covering her entire body except for her face, where the hood had fallen off.
His eyes were black; they seemed darker.

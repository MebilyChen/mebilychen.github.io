This is a story about a missing lover.

(())

He…
Here is a picture of me and him… His birthday just passed… He doesn’t like selfies…
He was cute, he was gentle, he won’t go near noisy crowds, nor walk too far alone…
His body was pure white and will glow in the dark…
He was wearing a light grey robe with dark stripes…
He always carried a cool smell of sandalwood that anyone could instantly recognize…
He rarely smiled. He was silent, always so grim…
Please, find him…
I don’t know what happened, we were having dinner… We were talking, when… He just disappeared, right in front of me… I spent the whole night looking for him——
Officer, I have nowhere to go! They don’t believe me——
Please…

This is a picture of me and him.
He won’t go near noisy crowds, nor will he walk too far alone.
He was pure white and wore a pale grey robe with dark stripes.
He had an aroma of sandalwood.
He…

(())

Lurien sat at the table clutching the photo, in the same spot where he had watched Wyrm disappear. He stared at the telephone in front of him, motionlessly, as if he was in a ritual. The food on the table was already cold, a sour smell lingered in the air. Flying insects settled on mushrooms and cutlets, the soup had solidified, a greasy, cold substance enveloping the spoon, and the grease floating on it cemented into stomach-churning shapes.
He looked across at the empty seat. Wyrm’s food was neatly cut into equal pieces, his knife still in its proper place, but the fork was dropped down on the floor, a few grease stains splattered on the carpet beside it.
“I——“ It caught them all by surprise. At the stroke of the hour, Wyrm’s eyes were suddenly alarmed, his last words choked off with the clanging of dropping forks, and the next moment there were only scattered light orbs left on the seat. Lurien pushed forward to grab the remnant in his vision, only to knock over his own soup bowl. While his brain stopped working, he felt a sudden calmness he had never felt before. It seemed as if nothing had happened, as if the other person had merely bid him a polite goodnight and left the room. He whispered his lover’s name, grabbed his coat and rushed out.
Lurien spent the night searching, along the road from east to the west, and around the riverbed from west to the east. He went to Wyrm’s favorite café , where he ate honey cupcakes; He went to the bar Wyrm used to go to when he was upset about him, where their every make up begun; He went to the little embankment where they used to go, where Wyrm liked to lie on the soft grass and nap.
Lurien always thought they were merely an ordinary couple, looking forward to the next day; Wyrm would bow his head and pucker up his mouth, saying he may take him back to his hometown, a small northern fishing village with permanent snow and ice.
When the telephone rang in front of him, he immediately grabbed the receiver. “Any progress?”
The woman on the other side hesitated, “Uh… I’m sorry, but there’s so little to go on that I can’t find anything.”
“You mean a person can just disappear from the world?”
“No, of course he didn’t disappear, it’s just… You haven’t been contacted? Asking for money or something?”
“No.”
“… You’d better prepare for the worst… Sir -“
“Only 14 hours! Impossible! It’s such a short time I can still ——“
“Sir, please calm down –”
“How can I calm down? How am I supposed to calm down when he’s still in the middle of nowhere? !”
“Sir, you must believe that we all want the same thing. We all want him to be safe.”
“……”
“I just want to make sure that you’re not holding anything back?”
“I…” Lurien lowered his head and clenched his fist. “He wasn’t kidnapped… He… He did disappear.”
“What?”
“No one believes me. I have to do this.”
“… I believe you, Sir. Go on. ‘
“He suddenly vanished, as if his own existence had been formless and dispersed…”
“……”
“You’ve got to believe me, you’ve seen his photo, he does exist——“
“Sir, I believe you, but this situation is beyond my power… However there may be one other place…”

(())

“… A God?”
“Yes. He is a god. According to your description, this is the only one explanation.” As the Seer cleaned the bell from her wooden cane, a musical sound chimed.
“Then…”
“Gods can not die, but there are ways to ‘kill’ them ——“
“Wyrm can’t die? Then where is he now? How can I find him? Why didn’t he come home?”
“Be patient, young man,” said the Seer with a glance. “The way to kill them is to destroy the Anchor they have left on earth, to make their Soul homeless and their vessel unformed.”
“… I, I did’t understand.”
“Someone destroyed his Anchor, so he disappeared.”
“… How do I find the Anchor?”
“You’ll have to ask him himself. Gods put their most important things where they think is the safest.”
“But ——“
“That’s as far as I can help you, boy.” She turned away from Lurien’s face. “… Is he your lover?”
“… Yes.”
“You have to be ready. Even if you did find his Anchor, there’s nothing you can do about it. Faith, once broken, can not be restored.”
“…”
“But, like I always said, Gods can not die.”

(())

“They’re just children! “the woman screamed.”They don’t understand anything! “
“They murdered someone. They killed Wyrm.”
“No! Don’t shoot, please, I’ll do anything to fix it! “
Lurien stood at the mouth of the cave with his shotgun, flakes of snow melting on his weather-beaten face. “… Tell me why… Why destroy him?”
“They were just kids, they were just trying to use the statue as a shooting target, they didn’t mean to do it, it was just an accident!”
Lurien went into the cave and loaded the gun.
“No, no! Help! Help! Someone please help us!”
“That’s how you load your guns, wasn’t it? One bullet at a time, laughing, joking and saying how you would like to kill him? Do you feel joy? Do you feel empowered? Knowing that you could easily kill another being?”
“Please forgive us, we’ve tried our best to repair it, but——“
“You know very well you can’t go into this cave. Why?”
“They just ——“
Lurien fired a shot into the ground, causing the other three to scream.
“I want to hear them say it!”
The woman sobbed and hurriedly pushed her eldest son. “We… Just curious… The elders always said this place was off limits, that it is sacred, but… That night, the ice that sealed the entrance to the cave suddenly melted… so we…”
“Why?”
“We are truly sorry mister, we promise we won’t do it again…”
Lurien silently looked into their tearful eyes and slowly lowered his gun. He turned towards the broken stone statue behind them, eaten beyond repair by mining picks. What should have been the sleeping face of a god is barely recognizable now. The stone shell was an unremarkable gray black, reflecting the leaping flames of the torch; The exposed metal core was as white as snow, as pure as Wyrm himself, emitting a cold smell of sandalwood. That was Wyrm’s Anchor. He walked right towards it. The mother and her sons panicked, immediately crowded into a corner, and the pickaxe in their hands was thrown aside.
The cold wind brought a piece of parchment from a corner to his feet. It was a leaflet on how to break the “Winter’s Seal” and mine the “Pale Ore”. On the night of the lunar eclipse on February 27, eight o ‘clock, launch the artillery to break the seal of the Winter God, all in one second. Afterwards, wait until the sun rose again, dig the ore with special pickaxes.
That was the night he disappeared.
He stared at the parchment in his hand.”It wasn’t an accident at all ——“
The wind and snow grew fiercer and moaned at the mouth of the cave. The hail mixed with the hexagonal ice crystals crashed into the cave, echoing. Lurien was just about to turn around and look, suddenly he heard a gunshot and a scream from behind him. He looked down, his clothes stained with blood.
The mother immediately hugged the trembling children and covered their eyes.
Lurien turned to return fire with his shotgun, but a bullet hit him right between the eyes.

Forgive me my God. He repented. For I didn’t even realize you were dying.

Part of him splashed across the white, broken idol of his beloved, and the rest of his body fell backward at the statue’s feet.
“Gods do not exist,” the man said,”It’s about time these mad cultists realized that. If it weren’t for our young generation’s foresight, this village wouldn’t have been like this today.”
The mother and her sons stepped across Lurien’s body and hurried to the man’s side, “… What took you so long! ? We were scared to death by this man!”
“Take this gun for protection. I’ll alarm the others ——“
“No… I don’t think anyone will come again… Anyway, how much did you sell those stones?”
The man smiled. “Enough to build us another house.”
The children seemed excited, “Daddy, I want a new little plane!” “I want a new train!” The man crouched down and fondly petted his sons’ heads. “No problem, daddy will buy you whatever you want.” He took two necklaces from his pocket and put them on his sons. These were two tiny figures carved from the Pale Ore. “Take these. Maybe they really have powers——if there are gods.”
“… What to do with the body?” the mother looked warily into that dark corner.
The storm outside is gradually subsiding. “I’ll go make a fire and burn it later.”

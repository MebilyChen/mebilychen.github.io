【Compilation】 Something Here and There

The Pale King Lurien The Watcher The Hollow Knight Quirrel Hornet

So basically I have nothing to say about this I was just playing around, yea.

“This won’t be an easy task,both physical and spiritual,Lurien.”
“It’s my duty,Your Majesty.And it is also my honor to serve you and your kingdom.”
“…You are allowed to refuse,I can find another bug.”
“We both know you can’t,my King,no offense.”
“But...”
“You gave me enteral life.Now it is time for me to return your favor,my King.”
“...Lurien,you still have a city to look after.”
“I believe your Majesty will find a qualified bug to replace me...and...”
Through his mask,Lurien looked at his dearest King with a little sadness.
“...and I’m sorry if I make you suffer.But sacrifice needs to be made.”
Lurien gave his King a deep bow.

“Why must Miss Monomon?!If she’s gone then how are you going to do with this archive and all her students?!”
“Manners,Quirrel!You are speaking to your King!”
Monomon scolded hurshly.She seldom scolds.
“I do not care! A king won’t ask his people to die!”
“Quirrel! That’s enough——”
“——Monomon,I would like to speak with you alone.”
But before Monomon could say anything, Quirrel step in front of his teacher.
“And I won’t let you.”
“Quirrel,go back to your dormitory!”
Quirrel didn’t reply, he just stood there and stared at the King with determination.
With no one saying any word, the atmosphere quickly became embarrassing.
“......Please,Your Majesty, please don’t hurt Miss Monomon...”
A small voice——nearly inaudible——suddenly broke the silence.
That was from a little girl standing by the door. One of Monomon’s students.
He almost said yes.
“Tera——”
“And do not hurt Quirrel!”Another voice cried,this time sounds like a little boy.
“All of you,go back to your room,now!”
“We will protect Miss Monomon and Quirrel!”An older boy shouted.
“Impenitent! Quirrel,this is your last chance. Leave and bring others along with you, or you will be no longer permitted to enter this archive.”
“That’s not fair to Quirrel!”
“Same punishments will come to you as well,if you don’t behave, students.”
“Why...?”Quirrel tried his best to restrain an urge to cry.
“...If you go now,I promise to explain to you later.”
“...You...you will...die...I...I can’t...”He began to sob.
“Everything will be fine, I promise. I never let you down,do I?”
That was when Quirrel started crying.
“Now go, my child.”
Quirrel stood for a moment,then he suddenly ran off the room.

“Wyrm?”
The Queen noticed his arrival, giving him a questioning look.
And the little beast —— the King would like to call her by that —— upon seeing him, quickly hide behind her second mother.
The King nodded slowly.
“I just drop by and need to make sure everything is going well.”
“Oh.”
The Queen then turned to look at that scared child.
“Come here.There‘s no need to fear, my child.He is your father, after all.”
The child poked her head out a little bit to get a more clearly view of her father.
The Queen let out a sigh.
“It’s understandable,though.For all these four years you haven’t seen her once.”
Her gaze was sad and slightly angry.
“You know it well,my Wyrm.Although you always say it’s just a bargain,she is still your child.”
The King stare at that little beast for a while,before he turned his gaze back to his White Lady.
“What’s her name?”
The Queen blinked.
“I can not name her. I’m not her parent.You are.You can give her a name now.”
“I don’t have time for this.”
“Wyrm,It’s just a name.”
“When she is old enough,send her to the Hive.”
“Wyrm,please,you can’t...”
“Queen Vespa have agreed to train her skill. I would like her becoming a guardian in the future——”
He paused for a moment,as if thinking.
“——she may become a Plan B as well.”
Then the King turned toward the doorway.
“...Let Vespa decide what she will be called then.”
“Please act as a father.”
“...I have a whole kingdom to look after,”
He started walking away.
“And she’s all yours now.”
“Wyrm—”
But he disappeared behind the palace’s gate.
“...I apologize if we scared you,my child.”
The white Lady spoke softly.
At the same time,the child finally came out from her hiding place.
“...Your father...he has lots of affairs to attend to.”
She closed her eyes.
“And he is scared,too.He scares he will lose you.”
The child stare at her,didn’t quite understand.
“I know my words are pale.But he...has already endured many things...too many things...those tragedies nearly tore his heart apart.
“I ask you to forgive him,my child.You may cannot understand,but he loves you...at least loved you. He is just...self-protecting.He can’t be held back by emotions.He must move forward and make sacrifice...for Hallownest and his people.”
The child still looked at her with her innocent eyes,still,full of wonder and hope.

The Knight was waiting outside,patiently.
The Pale King stood for a while,and called out.
“Come.”
The Knight acted immediately.
They stared at each other for another while.
“Are you scared?”
...No....
Their voice didn’t travel through the air,for they can’t actually speak.Instead,because the special bond they shared,their thoughts will instantly appear in their father’s head.
“...Do you know what it means to be a Hollow Knight?”
They hesitated for about a second,before shaking their head.
The Pale King sighed silently.
“It’s time for you to recognize your duty and choose your path.
“You are going to seal the old light.Defeat her,and keep her within your body forever...”
He paused.
“...and I know how it feels...to exposed in that blinding light...I won that fight,as I swallowed her.I thought I can seal her, using my body. I was wrong.That was a ridiculous attempt.”
Suddenly,his voice cracked a little.
“I was...She tortured me.With her fierce light,she cut through my bones and veins,burnt my skin,destroyed every single cell...bit by bit.”
He took a deep breath.
“...I will never forget that feeling,that slow and painful death she gave to me.
“Eventually,we died together.”
The Knight listened carefully and remained silent.
The King looked straight into their eyes——they have no eyes though,replacing by two eye-sockets——as if want to read their mind.
...But they are supposed to have no mind to think,right?
Only void and emptiness should exist.
“I died,and I revived.But you...you are not given the choice to die,you don’t have the power to gain rebirth neither.You are created to be immortal,a vessel to contain that light forever.You will be left alone,far away from the kingdom,enduring endless pain.
“Now answer me,are you scared?”
......
The King was waiting,waiting for a yes.
...Yes...but...no.
“......What do you mean by that?”
...Can’t...let you...die...
The King frowned a little.
“We are not talking about me, but you.”
...Protect...
“...You are willing to do this?”
...Duty....
No.Not again.
“...What if I say it is not your duty anymore now?”
...not...matter...love...father...
“Ah.Love.”
So the Void did lie to him.
The reason for them to obey him isn’t obedience,but love.
“...You will be abandoned alone, never seeing me again.”
...fine...you alive...fine...
“This is not that simple,little——”
He suddenly realized his little one is not “little” anymore.Actually now they are twice taller than him.So he must look up to match their gaze.
“——This is not that simple.”
...hurt?...
The King tensed.
“What did you say?”
...you...hurt?...
“No.”
He replied without a second thought.
...felt...pain...
Not again.
“I’m good.”
So familiar.He had experienced this before.

“If Lady Monomon’s sacrifice is a must, I’m willing to replace her in the position.”Quirrel said evenly, eyeing the ground, “though I doubt whether Your Grace’s plan demands that much, complicated enough to announce a request asking for a widely-beloved teacher’s demise.”
Lurien gave him a quick look.
“Please don’t, he’ll be confused.”He whispered.
“...Pardon?”
Lurien didn’t respond, instead, he turned to the King, who standing straight and calm, without any emotion revealed, judging.
“Please forgive my boldness, Your Majesty, ”he started, “but I insist that Quirrel shouldn’t stay in the Palace. ”
“I didn’t-”
“I’ll explain later.Please.”He whispered again, barely opened his mouth.
The King dipped his head slightly.
Lurien recognized his gesture, as a sign asking further explanation.“Since the research led by the Teacher still requires labour, I believe Quirrel would be the best candidate among all of them.”
Quirrel had heard of it, that Watcher Lurien seems to have a mysterious closed relationship with the King. “He knew the King more than the King himself did.”They’d say.
He’s smart, or he won’t be able to accompany the King for this long. Along with the Queen, Lurien is at the central point of the King’s trusted circle.
Maybe that’s why he accepted the advice without much consideration.“You shall have full authority on this, Lurien. The principle is to keep everything in balance.”

“And Gods don’t need emotions, they don’t possess them as well.”
“That’s not new.”
“What I am trying to say is, that, is the reason why His Majesty would be confused, if you bring that specific love-related topic to him.”

“He’s suffering. He didn’t say nothing but I knew. Those unfamiliar, boiled-up emotions inside him burnt him hard, and he doesn’t know how to put the fire off.”
He lowered his head, “For all these years, I had been trying to seek for a conversation. For him to realize emotions are acceptable, especially when he dwells in a mortal body. He didn’t want to reveal them to others.”
“You all think he is strong. Impossible to break, and also, cold-hearted.He was, but not anymore. He’s lonely, always alone. He called me once, to his chamber, asked me why he was feeling tired and ill with no reason at all. He just can’t understand what’s going on within himself. I tried my best to accompany him, as I decided to accept the room in the Palace which He provided. It’s not enough. He is still so lonely. Yet some of you made the situation even worse, pushing him towards the edge of the cliff without noticing. Every harsh word you said, every disgust you showed to him, they made him felt angry, and sad.”

“Master Lurien? His Majesty expects your presence in his chamber.”
“I’ll be there in no time.”He turned back to Quirrel.
“Maybe he made mistakes. But do not forget who brought you the opportunity to stand here and judge.” Lurien quickly finished the conversation, and hurried to the door.

“Traitor. You usurped your King.”
“Is that all you want to say?”
“I admire you as the princess. For this reason I won’t send an assassin.”
“You should try.”
Lurien simply narrowed his eyes, uninfected by her taunt. And Hornet hates this— A trace of her so-called father, always judging , cold, emotionless.
“He’s gone. He’s dead.”
“He’s not.”
“Find him then.”
“I’m working on it.”
“To tell you the truth, I don’t want to be a Queen at all.”

It’s hard to see him like this, wandering around the city streets, alone.
How many times did he act like this?
Lurien hid himself in the shadow of a nearby building and held his breath.
The rain kept falling, and he kept walking.
He knew him well, he didn’t want to be bothered when he was alone, and also, he was very sensitive. The conclusion is, if he dared to move a bit, he would be in trouble.
The King stopped.
But instead looking towards him, he raised his sight to the fake sky.
He stated humming.
Honestly, Lurien was shocked. He never thought He was interested in music.
It was a light-hearted tune, rather merrily. Not bad.
Now if he spots me I will be so dead. Lurien thought bitterly.
You won’t.
His voice suddenly echoed in his mind.
“Your Majesty-”
He absent-mindedly said the word out loud.

“I normally do not explain. Explanation is a way to ask for permission, and I do not need anyone’s approval for doing things.”
“...There are exceptions indeed, only when...someone was assisting, or my court discussing.”

“My flame seeks to melt you down, it desires a dance of Ice and Fire, of pure grace and vileness. ”He whispered to the King, gently, yet firmly took his claw, “Will you bless me with your presence in the performance, Your Majesty?”

——“The Wyrmly Love: A dance of Ice and Fire”
